Comprehensive overview of acne treatments from over-the-counter to professional procedures

üéØ Topical Treatments

Products applied directly to the skin to treat acne at the surface level.

OTC

Salicylic Acid

Exfoliates inside pores, reduces blackheads and whiteheads. Use in cleansers, toners, and spot treatments.

OTC

Benzoyl Peroxide

Kills acne-causing bacteria, reduces inflammation. Start with 2.5% concentration.

Prescription

Retinoids

Unclog pores, reduce inflammation. Includes tretinoin, adapalene, tazarotene.

üíä Oral Medications

Systemic treatments that work from within the body to control acne.

Prescription

Antibiotics

Reduce bacteria and inflammation. Doxycycline, minocycline for moderate-severe acne.

Prescription

Hormonal Treatments

Birth control pills, spironolactone for hormonal acne in women.

Prescription

Isotretinoin

Powerful treatment for severe, resistant acne. Requires medical supervision.

üî¨ Professional Procedures

In-office treatments performed by dermatologists for stubborn or severe acne.

Procedure

Chemical Peels

Exfoliate skin, unclog pores, reduce acne and improve texture.

Procedure

Laser Therapy

Reduce bacteria, decrease oil production, improve acne scars.

Procedure

Extraction & Drainage

Professional removal of large cysts and comedones to prevent scarring.

üåø Lifestyle & Prevention

Daily habits and skincare practices to prevent and manage acne.

Gentle Cleansing

Clean twice daily with mild, pH-balanced cleanser. Avoid over-washing.

Moisturizing

Use oil-free, non-comedogenic moisturizers to maintain skin barrier.

Sun Protection

Daily SPF 30+ to prevent post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation.

Diet & Stress

Balanced diet, manage stress, adequate sleep for overall skin health.
